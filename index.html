Thao P. Pham

PhD Candidate, Education Policy Studies · University of Wisconsin–Madison

I am a PhD candidate in Education Policy Studies at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. My research examines educational inequality across space and time — asking why gaps persist, where they concentrate, and who disappears inside aggregate categories.

I work at the intersection of quantitative history, spatial analysis, and causal inference. My first research strand uses spatial econometrics and Bayesian methods to examine how Confucian institutional legacies shape educational investment and attainment across Vietnamese provinces. My second strand studies Vietnamese, Cambodian, Lao, and Hmong communities in the United States, England, and France — populations that education data routinely collapses into a single “Asian American” or “Asian” category — asking how Confucian heritage and refugee experience jointly shape educational trajectories across national contexts.

Before Madison, I studied Public Policy at Indiana University Bloomington and Economics at the University of Manchester, UK.
